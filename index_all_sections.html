About Me

I started making websites as a hobby 15 years ago, teaching myself HTML, CSS, and PaintShop Pro (and eventually moved to Photoshop) through online tutorials. From 2008 - 2012, I attended University of Michigan - Ann Arbor and pursued a Bachelors of Science in Computer Engineering. I continued making websites and promotional graphics for student organizations throughout college, but took a break after starting to work. After working for 5 combined years in QA and project coordination positions, I realized that I missed the unique combination of creative and logical outlet that web development provided me. In 2018, I decided to enroll at Washtenaw Community College to pursue an Associate of Applied Science in Web Development to quantify my self-learning knowledge and learn how to approach web development from a professional standpoint. I will finish that degree in May 2019 with a 4.0 GPA and a couple additional advanced certifications in: Web Database Programming and Digital Strategy.

My father was an entrepreneur and an immigrant, owning multiple business while I was growing up. The longest running business was a restaurant, where I started working as a cashier from 9 years old, eventually becoming a server in my high school years to save money for college. My many years at the restaurant taught me essential people and customer service skills. Meanwhile, assisting my father with administrative and managerial tasks built my leadership skills and instilled me with an attention to detail.

Cyber security has always been an interest of mine. I entered a competition hosted by University of Notre Dame in 2007 and was fortunate enough to get my paper published in 2008. The paper was about the, then still new, topic of cyber attack on government agencies.
